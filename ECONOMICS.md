# Economics

## What Is a Lead Worth?

If Credex helps a startup reduce AI infrastructure costs through discounted credits, a converted customer could reasonably generate a few hundred to several thousand dollars in annual value depending on team size.

For this estimate, I assume:
- average small team size: 5–15 users
- recurring AI spend: $200–$2000/month
- Credex earns value through credit resale margins or partnerships

Estimated lead value:
- approximately $300–$1500 annually for a converted active customer

---

# Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

The early acquisition strategy relies mostly on:
- organic social sharing
- Reddit communities
- Indie Hackers
- Product Hunt
- founder communities

Because these channels are mostly free, early CAC could remain relatively low.

Estimated early CAC:
- $5–$20 per qualified lead

This could increase significantly if paid ads are introduced later.

---

# Conversion Assumptions

Estimated funnel:

1000 visitors
→ 250 completed audits
→ 40 consultation requests
→ 8 paying conversions

This assumes:
- strong audit usefulness
- noticeable savings recommendations
- relevant startup audience

---

# What Makes This Profitable?

The tool becomes profitable if:
- the cost of generating an audit remains low
- consultation conversion remains healthy
- average customer value exceeds acquisition costs

For example:

8 customers × $800 annual value
= $6400 potential yearly value

If acquisition costs remain below that amount, the model is sustainable.

---

# Path to $1M ARR

To approach $1M ARR within 18 months, the product would likely need:
- several thousand active audits monthly
- strong organic sharing loops
- integration into startup communities
- recurring high-value business customers

Example rough model:

1500 paying customers
× ~$700 yearly value
≈ ~$1M ARR

This would require significantly stronger infrastructure, analytics, and outbound sales support than the current prototype.
