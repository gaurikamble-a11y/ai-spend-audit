# Metrics

## North Star Metric

Completed audits per week.

The core value of the product is helping users identify unnecessary AI spending. A completed audit indicates that a user successfully interacted with the tool and received value from it.

At the current stage, completed audits are a more meaningful metric than total visitors because the product is focused on utility rather than passive traffic.

---

# Supporting Metrics

## 1. Audit Completion Rate

Percentage of visitors who complete the audit form after landing on the website.

This helps measure:
- landing page clarity
- form usability
- user intent quality

A low completion rate would indicate friction in the onboarding flow or unclear value proposition.

---

## 2. Average Estimated Savings

Average monthly savings identified per completed audit.

This helps evaluate:
- usefulness of recommendations
- quality of pricing logic
- potential lead quality for Credex

If the average identified savings are consistently low, the recommendation engine may need improvement.

---

## 3. Share Rate

Percentage of completed audits that are shared publicly or sent to others.

Because the product is naturally screenshot-friendly, sharing behavior could become an important organic growth signal.

---

# Early Instrumentation Plan

The first analytics events I would track are:

- landing page visits
- audit started
- audit completed
- reset clicked
- share clicked
- consultation CTA clicked

This would help identify where users drop off during the audit flow.

---

# Pivot Signal

If fewer than 10–15% of visitors complete the audit consistently over several weeks, I would revisit:
- landing page messaging
- form complexity
- recommendation quality

A low completion rate would suggest the tool is not clearly communicating value quickly enough.

---

# Long-Term Thinking

As the product grows, the most important business metric would likely become:

completed audits → consultation requests → converted customers

However, at the current prototype stage, successful audit completion is the clearest signal of product usefulness.
